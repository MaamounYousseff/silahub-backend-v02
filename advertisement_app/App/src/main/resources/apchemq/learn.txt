For consumers, it works a little differently because Spring manages listener containers, not JmsTemplate.
The listener container creates one or more JMS sessions depending on its configuration.
Sessions are long-lived, not created per message.
Each session has its own consumer (MessageConsumer) attached to the queue or topic.
